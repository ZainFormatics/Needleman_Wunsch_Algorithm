Pairwise Alignment

Global alignment is a computational technique used in bioinformatics and sequence analysis to compare two sequences, typically DNA, RNA, or protein sequences.

The goal of global alignment is to identify the optimal alignment of the entire length of two sequences, considering similarities and differences between them.

One of the most well-known algorithms for global alignment is the Needleman-Wunsch algorithm.
